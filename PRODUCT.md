# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Sparkly Tails store staff, not customers. Opened from within Shopify admin — mostly the Shopify mobile app on a shared iPad at the sales counter, sometimes a phone or desktop browser. Used mid-interaction: a customer is standing at the counter picking up their pet food subscription, and staff need to quickly see what's left on the order, mark each item picked, skipped, or swapped, and move on. Not a leisurely browsing context — short, repeated, task-focused sessions between customers.

## Product Purpose

Replaces a manual Google Sheets tracker for an in-store pet food subscription pickup service. Lets staff confirm which subscription items a customer picked up, track substitutions and skips, and automatically fire a Klaviyo confirmation email, without updating a spreadsheet by hand. Success looks like: staff can confirm a pickup in a few taps with no training or explanation needed, and the data stays accurate enough to trust for follow-up emails and restocking decisions.

## Brand Personality

No strong external references requested — the explicit direction is clean and fast. This is a utilitarian tool that should disappear into the task rather than assert a visual identity. Three words: efficient, quiet, unfussy.

## Anti-references

Avoid consumer-app flourishes — marketing-style hero sections, decorative illustration, heavy motion, gradient or glass effects — that would slow down a staff member trying to finish a task quickly. Also avoid dense enterprise-dashboard clutter; this isn't a data-analysis tool, it's a handful of simple screens for a repetitive, physical task.

## Design Principles

- Speed over polish — every screen scannable and actionable in a couple of seconds
- One primary action per screen — nothing to hunt for
- Tap-friendly for a shared iPad — generous touch targets, not desktop-density UI
- Fail visibly, not silently — if the app can't be used (e.g. opened outside Shopify admin), say so plainly instead of a blank or broken screen
- No decoration for its own sake — every visual element earns its place

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard good practice: readable contrast, reasonable tap target sizes for a shared iPad used quickly between customers, and pickup status (picked/skipped/swapped) distinguishable without relying on color alone. No individual accessibility needs specified beyond this.
